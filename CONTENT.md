## Section — The Real Reason Couples Drift
- Headline: **It's not the fight. It's the *pattern.***
- Sub: Most couples don't drift apart because they lack information. They drift because they get caught in patterns they cannot see.
- Escalating lines:
  - A look becomes **a story.**
  - A silence becomes **rejection.**
  - A question becomes **criticism.**
  - A need becomes **pressure.**
  - A disagreement becomes **danger.**
- Closing: Before either person realizes what happened, the marriage is no longer being led by love. It's being led by old wounds, inherited roles, and survival strategies formed long before the relationship began.

## Section — The Core Truth
- Headline: **Past patterns predict the future — *until awareness interrupts the loop.***
- Sub: A spouse doesn't respond to what happened. They respond to what their lens says it means. That's why two people can live the same moment and experience two entirely different realities.
- Formation sequence: Event → Meaning → Feeling → Decision → Lens → Pattern

## Section — Guide
- Quote: "One partner says, 'I just needed space.' The other hears, 'I do not matter.'"
- We don't diagnose each other: This isn't about winning arguments with better language, or labeling your partner. It's about finally understanding the hidden emotional logic beneath your reactions.
- We slow the moment down: The facilitator doesn't teach at you. They help you see what's happening between you, slow the pattern down, and practice a different response while the moment is still alive.

## Section — The Experience (Five Capacities)
- Headline: **Five capacities you *live*, not memorize.**
- Sub: These aren't principles to study. They're capacities couples build by practicing them in the room.
- 1. Emotional Safety — Learn to lower the emotional temperature before trying to solve the issue.
- 2. Pattern Awareness — Stop asking "What's wrong with us?" and start asking "What pattern are we in?"
- 3. Lens Fluency — Understand the lens beneath the behavior, so you respond with clarity instead of blame.
- 4. Repair & Return — Come back together faster, cleaner, and with less damage after rupture.
- 5. Shared Creation — Leave with a living marriage agreement built around safety, awareness, and repair.

## Section — Lens Fluency (Eight Lenses)
- Headline: **Every spouse sees through *a lens.***
- Sub: Each began as a survival strategy — a way to stay safe, loved, valued, or in control. In marriage, that strategy quietly becomes a driver.
- Savior — "I am valuable when I rescue or help."
- Survivor — "I am safe when I protect myself."
- Prover — "I am worthy when I perform."
- Martyr — "I am loved when I sacrifice."
- Analyst — "I am safe when I understand and control."
- Endurer — "I am strong when I suffer well."
- Ghost — "I am safe when I disappear."
- Chameleon — "I am accepted when I adapt."

## Section — How Each Session Moves
- Headline: **Not a lecture. An *experience.***
- Movement sequence: Story → Recognition → Experience → Reflection → Practice → Agreement
- Sub: You don't simply learn concepts. You apply them in real time, with your partner, through guided reflection, facilitated dialogue, and pattern-aware practice — and leave able to do things differently.

## Arc line
- From two people caught in patterns they cannot see — to two people who see, soften, repair, and create together.

## Safety note
- A note on safety
- This is not a rescue program for unsafe or destructive marriages. Where there is abuse, coercion, addiction, chronic contempt, or danger, safety and professional intervention come first. This experience is for couples who are both willing to grow — and ready to look honestly at themselves.
